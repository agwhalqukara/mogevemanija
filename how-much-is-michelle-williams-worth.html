Michelle Williams career

Williams is best known for appearing in small-budget independent films with somber or tragic themes. She has won numerous honors, including two Golden Globe Awards, a Primetime Emmy Award, five Academy Award nominations, and a Tony Award.

Williams, a politician and trader’s daughter, started her career doing cameos on television before making her feature debut in the 1994 family picture Lassie.

At the age of fifteen, she was freed from her parents’ control and quickly rose to fame for her starring part as Jen Lindley in the teen drama series Dawson’s Creek (1998–2003). After that, she starred in a few low-key movies until making her debut in the thriller Brokeback Mountain (2005).

Williams’ performances as emotionally damaged women dealing with bereavement or loneliness in the indie dramas Manchester by the Sea (2016), Blue Valentine (2010), and Wendy and Lucy (2008) won her praise from critics.

For her roles as Gwen Verdon in the miniseries Fosse/Verdon (2019) and Marilyn Monroe in the drama My Week with Marilyn (2011), she was awarded two Golden Globes and a Primetime Emmy Award.

The mystery movie Shutter Island (2010), the fantasy movie Oz the Great and Powerful (2013), the musical The Greatest Showman (2017), and the superhero movies Venom (2018) and Venom: Let There Be Carnage (2021) were her highest-grossing releases.

Williams has also helmed big-budget studio productions, including Steven Spielberg’s semi-autobiographical drama The Fabelmans (2022) and Ridley Scott’s criminal thriller All the Money in the World (2017).

Williams performed in Broadway revivals of two shows: the drama Blackbird (2016) and the musical Cabaret (2014). For the latter, she was nominated for a Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play.

She is a supporter of workplace equity in compensation. Williams, who has always maintained her privacy regarding her personal life, was briefly married to musician Phil Elverum and has a daughter from her relationship with actor Heath Ledger.
